Hello

After a few months of varying levels of success trading options, one of the bits of advice I kept coming across was to journal my trades. I started by just writing them down in a notebook, but eventually realized I wasn't going through them in order to learn. I moved over to taking notes in excel which consisted of the various stats on the trade and my emotions before, during, and after the trade was completed, in addition to what I may have learned from that trade. I wanted something that looked better and more inviting, more user friendly, and more robust than just Excel cells...needless to say, there wasn't much out there. Enter this website. It is my hope that this site will enable me to become a better trader by making my data, notes, and other information more accessible and encourage me to actively utilize the lessons I learn daily by trading options in the stock market.

My goal is to become emotionally, financially, mentally, and physically fit and content by focusing on diet, exercise, mediation, winning trades, retiring early, and most importantly spending quality AND quantity time with my daughter. It is my assesment that the best way to achieve these goals is to become an expert at Technical Analysis, Macroeconomic Event Analysis, Portfolio Risk Management, and Self Awareness through Trading Psychology.

Some of My Favorite Learning Resources

YouTube: Right Way Options, Simpler Trading, Adam Sliver Trade, Trading with Rayner among many others.

One of the best places to find a wealth of information, also a great deal of worthless or misinformation is YouTube. Provided that a person is able to sift through the garbage that leads them in the wrong direction, or no direction to be equally accurate, and find the right channels in which to learn to make their own correct decisions and make winning trades, YouTube is one of the greatest learning resources the world has ever known. Hopefully by providing some of my favorites here, I will remember to stop in and check on them regularly, but also provide a good start for those looking to take in some invaluable information.

Yahoo Finance

Yahoo Finance is a site that allows someone to search different stock tickers, news events, and general economic and stock market happenings. Any publicly traded company can be researched, along with some of the prominent financial reporters' stories being available to get a good understanding of projected market and specific stock predictions. Additionally, each stock ticker can have their chart pulled up to technical analysis for free.
